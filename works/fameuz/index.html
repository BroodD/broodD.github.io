About course

This online-program is designed to prepare the aspiring screenwriter for writing an original screenplay of feature film and understand how to sell it, by learning what Producers and Studios want, and more importantly, what they do not want.

This course includes 12 step-by-step tutorials and assignments, after completing those you will have created a complete and workable screenplay outline. It's 25% of the entire screenplay creation process.

You will learn and immediately apply new knowledge, and most importantly, you will have the opportunity to receive personal feedback from Ralph DiBacco for each of the 12 completed assignments and your screenplay idea in general.

What You'll
Learn:

  • How to cultivate an original screenplay idea in a logical progression from the firstinspiration to the final outline.
  • How to introduce and develop your protagonist and antagonist
  • How to plan a story using an outline and notecards
  • How to write memorable characters and surprising stories
  • How to recognize and avoid weak and cliché plots
  • How to use misdirection to add conflict for your protagonist
  • How to protect the copyright of your script
  • Which scripts are of interest to modern producers and studios, and how to sell your first script.

Who Should Take This Course:

  • Complete beginner who wants to become a screenwriter
  • Writers who want to understand the structure of feature film
  • Writers who want to understand the functions of each act in a feature film
  • Writers wanting to develop writing skills using targeted creative assignments
  • Writers looking to solidify structure in their screenplays
  • Writers stuck in the second act.
  • Writers seeking a satisfying ending for their screenplays.
  • Writers who wants tips for his/her script from a practicing Hollywood screenwriter.
About your teacher

Ralph W. DiBacco

Hollywood screenwriter and Industry professional with over 30 years of screenwriting and production experience. Teacher in Screenwriters University, Operated By F&W Media.

In 1994, Ralph received certification from Dov S-S Simens’ Hollywood Film Institute, joining such alumni as Queen Latifah, Will Smith, and Quentin Tarantino. That same year, he enrolled in the Hollywood Scriptwriting Institute, where he spent the next two years honing his craft under noted screenwriter Earl Blakesley, Jr., whose screen credits included the popular television shows China Beach and Tour of Duty.

Feedback from a practicing screenwriter

The course is divided into 2 packages, a basic (theoretical part of 12 video lessons and 12 tasks to create a plan for your future script) and advanced (checking tasks, getting feedback and recommendations from Ralph Dibacco).
After you complete and upload the task to our website, where the training will take place, teacher will check it and give detailed feedback within 3 days.

Here's what it looks like:

Lexi,

I was impressed with your breakdown of the key story elements. What I found particularly interesting were the parallels that you established between the historical killings and the main characters. For example, the fact that one (or more?) of the female characters are pregnant. This will surely add to viewer engagement.

I also like the introduction of the Professor character. He could be extremely valuable in balancing the “youthful” energy with a more “academic” approach. Besides, you will need a character or two to kill off early. He may fit that need perfectly.

Now, this is just a suggestion, but what if you developed the parallel elements in more detail. What if one of the main characters has blood-ties with one of the killer’s victims? And thus is related to the killer. Because of the parallel structure of the story, the conditions and relations of the characters may be enough to “trigger” a response from the killer’s spirit. Plus, it would make a possession seem more credible, if the character becoming possessed is a descendant of the killer. Just a thought.

I really like the direction that your story is headed. One other character that you may want to consider introducing (if you haven’t done so already) is an older individual who actually knew the family. This would be a great way to reveal backstory and exposition, as it would be organic to the story. Yes, the Professor and library research would serve that purpose. But, how much better would it be to have a first-hand eyewitness! I can’t remember if you mentioned when the original killings took place. But, any time within the past 70 years would be suitable for an eyewitness, albeit an elderly one.

Well Lexi, great job once again. Keep up the great work!
